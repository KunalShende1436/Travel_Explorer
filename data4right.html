Hampi

Geographic Loaction:

Hampi is located in the state of Karnataka, India. It is situated on the southern bank of the Tungabhadra River and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hampi Attractions:

  1. Virupaksha Temple:

    An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its towering gopuram and vibrant annual festival.

  2. Historical Significance:

    Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century and is renowned for its rich history, reflected in the well-preserved ruins of temples, palaces, and other structures.

  3. Vijaya Vittala Temple:

    Famous for its musical pillars and the iconic stone chariot, this temple is a masterpiece of Vijayanagara architecture.

  4. Architectural Marvels:

    Explore the impressive Virupaksha Temple, Vijaya Vittala Temple with its iconic stone chariot, and the Queen's Bath, showcasing the architectural grandeur of the Vijayanagara era.
